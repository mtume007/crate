Philosophy

HULDRA is a personal design system named after the Norwegian forest spirit — beautiful on the surface, precise and modular underneath. It is the design language for a suite of applications built around one belief: that software for people who care about their collections should feel like the collection itself. Considered. Calm. Timeless.

The UI is the room the collection lives in.
The room doesn't perform.

The shared principle across all three references: reduce until only the essential remains. Then refine what's left until it's beautiful.

Design principles

01

One font

Martian Mono throughout. Weight and size carry all hierarchy. Three weights: 300, 400, 500. The whole app speaks one typographic language.

02

Amber is structural

The accent appears only on chrome labels — YEAR, GENRE, BPM — and the wordmark. Never on content. Artist names are white. "2002" is muted. Absolute.

03

Nothing hardcoded

Every value lives in config. Every colour is a token. Swap the accent, toggle the mode — the whole system follows.

04

Artwork first

The heaviest visual element is the album cover, not the labels. Text recedes. Art comes forward. The grid exists to display covers, not to fill space.

05

Spring motion

Cards scale with a slight overshoot on hover. The UI is alive at the point of contact. Only transform and opacity — never layout.

06

Two modes

Dark is near-black #080808. Light is warm parchment #f5f0e8. The amber accent shifts to match. Same personality, different room.

07

Glass layers

Toolbar, titlebar, and playbar float above content with backdrop blur. The chrome recedes. The music comes forward.

08

Whitespace is structure

Padding and margin do the work of divider lines. Breathing room makes the layout feel intentional — like a well-set page in a book.

Weight hierarchy

Rams' Braun products use weight sparingly. The heaviest visual element is the product — the record player, the radio — not the labelling. In Crate, the heaviest element is the album artwork. Text recedes to support it.

300 Light All secondary text — sidebar items, artist names, counts, metadata values, section headers, playbar artist. The majority of the app lives here.
400 Regular Primary content — album titles, active sidebar items, toolbar title, track names, playbar track name. What you're looking at, not looking past.
500 Medium CRATE wordmark only. Nothing else in the app should be 500. When 500 appears, it's the mark, not the content.

Casing rules

Uppercase makes every word the same visual weight. Sentence case creates natural hierarchy — the eye moves through it, not across it as a wall of identical caps.

The test

Pass / fail criterion
When you open Crate, your eye should go to the album covers first. Not the sidebar, not the section headers, not the playbar. The covers.

Everything else should support that focus — quietly, structurally, beautifully. If any element of chrome is competing with the artwork for attention, it has failed. Remove it, lighten it, or shrink it until it stops.

Crate — the first HULDRA application

Crate is a local-first desktop music library for audiophiles, DJs, and collectors. Built for people who have spent years accumulating records, digital files, and rare pressings — and want a single place that treats that collection with the seriousness it deserves.

Not a streaming interface. Not a playlist manager. A library and a database — the kind of thing a serious collector would build for themselves if they could.

Scans local music folders, reads file tags, deduplicates intelligently
Enriches every album via Discogs — label, cat#, pressing, year, format
AI-assisted matching — Claude normalises queries, validates results
Review pipeline — auto-flags low-confidence matches for quick confirm or manual correction
Detail panel shows cat#, label, runtime alongside artwork
Single-album add flow — drop a folder, pick the pressing, done
Dark and light mode, fully themeable from config
409 albums · 4 564 tracks — and growing
